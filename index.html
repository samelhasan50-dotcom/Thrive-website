Mental Health

Your Mind Matters

Mental health is just as real as physical health. Here's what you need to know.

What is anxiety?

Anxiety is your body's natural alarm system — it's normal to feel it before a test or big event. But when worry feels constant and uncontrollable, it might be an anxiety disorder. About 1 in 3 teens experience this at some point.

Signs of depression in teens

More than just feeling sad — depression can look like irritability, losing interest in things you used to enjoy, low energy, trouble concentrating, or changes in sleep and appetite. It's treatable and you deserve support.

Stress vs. burnout

Stress is temporary pressure that eases when the problem goes away. Burnout is when you've been running on empty for too long — you feel exhausted, detached, and like nothing matters. Both are real and valid.

When to talk to someone

If your feelings are interfering with school, friendships, or daily life for more than two weeks — talk to a trusted adult, school counselor, or doctor. You don't have to hit a "low enough" point to deserve help.

Common Questions

Is it normal to feel anxious all the time as a teen?
Some anxiety is totally normal — teen years are full of big changes. But if worry is constant, stops you from doing things you want to do, or causes physical symptoms like headaches or stomachaches regularly, it's worth talking to someone about it.
How do I tell someone I'm struggling?
You don't need to have the "perfect" words. You could start with: "I've been having a hard time lately and I don't really know how to explain it, but I wanted to tell you." A trusted adult — parent, teacher, or school counselor — will take it from there.
What if no one believes me?
If the first person you tell dismisses you, try someone else. You can also call or text a crisis line (see Resources below) where trained counselors will always take you seriously. Your feelings are real and you deserve to be heard.
Physical Health

Your Body, Explained

Bodies change a lot during the teen years. Here's a calm, factual guide to what's going on.

🌱

Puberty & Growth

Hormones drive major changes in your body between ages 8–18. Everyone's timeline is different — early, late, or in between is all normal.

Ages 8–18
🛌

Sleep Science

Teens biologically shift to staying up later and sleeping later. You need 8–10 hours. Sleep affects mood, memory, metabolism, and growth.

8–10 hrs/night
🍽️

Nutrition Basics

Your brain and body need carbs, protein, fats, vitamins, and minerals. Skipping meals hurts focus and mood. No food is inherently "bad."

Fuel up
🏃

Exercise & Movement

60 minutes of movement a day is the guideline — but even 20 minutes of walking boosts mood, focus, and sleep quality significantly.

60 min/day
💧

Hydration

Even mild dehydration causes headaches, trouble focusing, and fatigue. Aim for about 8 cups of water a day — more if you're active.

8 cups/day
🩺

Check-ups Matter

Annual check-ups catch things early. Don't avoid doctors — they're on your side. You can also ask to speak to them privately.

Once a year
